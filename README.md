# PYTHON_MINI_PROJECT
# Customer Segmentation & RFM Analysis using Python

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on **Customer Segmentation and Revenue Analysis** using **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) Analysis** in Python.  
The objective was to analyze customer purchasing behavior, identify high-value customer segments, and generate business insights that can improve retention, engagement, and revenue growth strategies.

The analysis was conducted on:
- **1,000 unique customers**
- **23,050 transaction records**

---

# 🚀 Business Problem
Businesses often struggle to:
- Identify their most valuable customers
- Understand purchasing behavior
- Detect customers likely to churn
- Optimize marketing and retention strategies

This project solves these challenges using data-driven customer segmentation.

# 📈 Key Insights from the Analysis

## 🔹 Revenue Concentration Insight
The top **20% customers contributed approximately 27% of total revenue**, showing that the business revenue distribution is more balanced and does not strictly follow the traditional 80/20 Pareto principle.

---

## 🔹 Champion Customers
Only **35 customers** were categorized as **Champions**, but they showed:
- High purchase frequency
- High monetary value
- Recent engagement

These customers represent the most valuable customer segment for the business.

---

## 🔹 Big Spenders Segment
The **Big Spenders** segment consisted of around **165 customers** contributing a disproportionately high share of revenue compared to the average customer.

This indicates that a relatively small customer base drives a major portion of premium sales.

---

## 🔹 Customer Churn Insight
Approximately **34 customers** were identified as **Lost Customers**, indicating:
- Low recent activity
- Reduced transaction frequency
- Declining spending behavior

These users are potential churn cases requiring re-engagement strategies.

---

## 🔹 Spending Pattern Insight
Customers with:
- Lower recency values (recent purchases)
- Higher transaction frequency

generally demonstrated significantly higher monetary contribution.

This highlights a strong relationship between customer engagement and revenue generation.

---

## 🔹 Revenue Distribution Insight
The analysis showed that revenue generation is spread across multiple customer segments instead of depending heavily on only a few customers.

This indicates:
- Lower business dependency risk
- More stable customer revenue flow
